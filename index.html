The Beatles: The Band That Changed the World

The Beatles, formed in Liverpool in 1960, were an English rock band consisting of John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr. Widely regarded as the most influential band of all time, they were integral to the development of 1960s counterculture and popular music's recognition as an art form.

Historic Album

Released in 1967, Sgt. Pepper's is considered one of the greatest albums in music history.

Beatlemania

The Beatles sparked a global cultural revolution in the 1960s with massive fan hysteria and media coverage.

Legacy

Decades after their breakup, The Beatles’ influence continues across generations, genres, and disciplines.

Fun Facts

  • 20 No.1 hits on Billboard.
  • First stadium concert in history.
  • Innovators in music production.
